AIMENTORA · Content Blueprint

What it takes to clear the UPSC — and where your library stands

The Civil Services exam is won across three gates and roughly a dozen content pillars. This blueprint lays out what a serious aspirant needs at each one, measured against what you have today — so the next thing to build is never a guess.

1

The three gates

A candidate must pass each stage in turn, and each rewards a different kind of preparation. Content that wins one gate rarely wins the next — the library has to serve all three.

Gate 1 · Screening

Prelims

  • GS Paper I — 100 objective questions spanning 9 subjects
  • CSAT Paper II — qualifying, but ends many attempts
  • Rewards: wide factual recall, elimination skill, speed
  • Needs: huge MCQ volume, PYQs, current affairs, mocks
Gate 2 · The real exam

Mains

  • Essay + GS1–GS4 + Optional — 1750 marks, all written
  • Rewards: structured argument, not just knowledge
  • Won on answer writing, model frameworks, ethics cases
  • Needs: theme-wise notes, model answers, essay practice
Gate 3 · The finish

Interview

  • Personality Test — 275 marks on the DAF profile
  • Rewards: clarity, balance, awareness, temperament
  • Needs: DAF-based question sets, opinion frameworks
  • Later-stage: build once the core library is deep
